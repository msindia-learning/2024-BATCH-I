APJ Abdul Kalam, an eminent scientist and the 11th President of India, was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. Known as the "Missile Man of India," Kalam played a pivotal role in India's space and missile programs. His visionary leadership and contributions to science and technology earned him widespread respect and admiration. Beyond his scientific achievements, Kalam was a passionate advocate for education and youth empowerment, inspiring millions with his wisdom and humility. He passed away on July 27, 2015, leaving behind a legacy of innovation, integrity, and dedication to the progress of his nation and humanity
